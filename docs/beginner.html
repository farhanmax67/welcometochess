The Foundations

What is Chess?

Chess is a two-player strategy game played on an 8x8 board with 64 squares. Each player starts with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns. The goal is to checkmate the opponent's king.

The Chessboard

The board is arranged in an 8x8 grid. Squares alternate light and dark. A key rule: "white on right" â€” the bottom-right square for each player must be light. Vertical columns are 'files' (a-h) and horizontal rows are 'ranks' (1-8).

Setting Up the Board

Place the board so each player has a light square on their right. The back row from left to right: Rook, Knight, Bishop, Queen, King, Bishop, Knight, Rook. Front row: 8 pawns. The queen always starts on her own color.
